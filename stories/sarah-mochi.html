From Feathers to Fame: How Mochi Inspired a Purrfect Career

March 22, 2023 ‚Ä¢ 7 min read ‚Ä¢ Dreams Come True

When Sarah K. adopted Mochi through Purrfect Match, she thought she was just getting a shy, feather-obsessed companion. Little did she know, she was about to embark on a journey that would lead to designing costumes for Taylor Swift's cats and living out her wildest dreams.

"Mochi's profile mentioned she was obsessed with feathers," Sarah recalls. "I thought it was just a cute quirk. I had no idea it would change both of our lives forever."

"Sometimes the smallest paws leave the biggest footprints on your heart - and your career path."

The Feather That Started It All

After bringing Mochi home, Sarah noticed her new feline friend's fascination went beyond typical cat play. Mochi would study feathers with intense focus, batting them around with surgical precision and even arranging them in specific patterns on the floor.

Mochi's intricate feather arrangements

Sarah's early feather toy designs

Inspired by Mochi's passion, Sarah began creating elaborate feather toys. What started as simple DIY projects evolved into intricate, hand-crafted works of art. She started an Instagram account to share her creations, never expecting it to become anything more than a hobby.

When Swifties Discovered Mochi

One fateful day, a popular Swiftie account shared a video of Mochi playing with a feather toy designed to look like Taylor's "Lover" album aesthetic. The video went viral, catching the attention of Taylor Swift's team.

"I woke up to thousands of notifications," Sarah says. "Then I got an email that literally made me drop my phone. It was from Taylor's management team, asking if I'd be interested in creating custom pieces for Olivia Benson and Meredith Grey."

"Say you'll remember me, standing in a nice dress, staring at the sunset, babe"

- Taylor Swift, Wildest Dreams

Designing the Purr-eras Tour Collection

Sarah and Mochi were invited to collaborate on creating miniature versions of Taylor's iconic Eras Tour costumes for her feline companions. The project required meticulous attention to detail and countless hours of feather-work.

The "Folklore" inspired piece

"1989" sequined masterpiece

"Mochi was my quality control expert," Sarah laughs. "She'd inspect every feather, every sequin. If she approved with a gentle headbutt, I knew it was purrfect. If she walked away, I'd start over."

A Night to Remember

The big reveal happened backstage at one of Taylor's shows. Sarah and Mochi were invited to present the finished collection to Taylor and her cats.

"Taylor was so gracious and genuinely excited about the costumes. When Olivia Benson strutted in her 'Reputation' outfit and Meredith Grey posed regally in her 'Evermore' piece, we all knew it was magic. Mochi even got to play with the cats using some of the feather toys - it was like watching three tiny superstars."

The moment that made all the hard work worth it

Living Their Wildest Dreams

Today, Sarah runs a successful custom costume and cat toy business, with Mochi serving as Chief Inspiration Officer. They've been featured in major publications and even appeared on a popular daytime talk show.

"None of this would have happened without Purrfect Match," Sarah reflects. "That initial connection, that moment when I saw Mochi's profile mentioning her feather obsession - it set everything in motion. We truly made each other's wildest dreams come true."
